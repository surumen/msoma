About the M-Soma Bootcamp

Flipped Classroom

The bootcamp is an intensive and immersive four-week program designed to prepare beginners who have never done any programming to kick start a career in computer science. We apply a "flipped classroom" model where there are no lectures - just workshops and labs. You access workshop material before the day of instruction and work on projects during sessions.

Daily Schedule

There is one workshop in the morning, and two labs in the afternoon from Monday to Friday. Workshops are two hours long, with a break in between. On some days, there will be special guests we invite to inspire you with their work. Each labs is one and a half hour long. During labs, you work on individual projects with the help of our instructors and your peers.

Courses

Our courses are designed to give you the confidence and skills you need to be a great programmer. We cover courses ranging from Intro to Programming (taught in Python) to Web Development and Design Thinking. We also incorporate an ethics course to challenge you to think consciously about the social implications of using and developing technology.

What Happens Next?

Once you're admitted into the bootcamp, you become an M-Soma fellow forever. Scholars who wish to apply to colleges outside Kenya would get mentorship and advice on the application process from our committed community of mentors. Those who wish to venture into different paths will also be matched with mentors with similar interests.

Design Thinking

There will be four main courses covered during the bootcamp. We put emphasis on preparing you to be an independent learner who can apply computer science concepts to solve problems in our day to day life. Our programming course is not meant to teach any programming language, but rather to prepare you to be a great programmer who can easily pick up any programming language thereafter

The design thinking course is designed to equip you with the skills you will need as an entrepreneur and in absolutely any other field. It's a way of solving problems that is human-centered. More details on design thinking on the course page linked above.
The web development course will enable you to build your portfolio and showcase your projects by the end of the four weeks

What Next?

After the bootcamp, we will continue to support you in your journey to becoming a skilled software developer, or any path you choose to take

Want to Join College?
We will guide you through the application process and assist you in preparing for your university entry examinations if applicable. We will also try as much as possible to match you with a mentor who is studying in the university you're applying to.

Working on your own Projects?
M-Soma aims to create technology leaders who would create jobs in their sectors. If that's your goal at any point in your life - not just immediately after the bootcamp - we will provide you with mentorship and the support you need to get started. We will help you draft a plan for your business, and connect you with the people and organizations that might help you along the way.

Any Others
The wonderful thing about our bootcamp is that we will get to know you individually, and hopefully help you discover your passion and the direction you want to take in your life. Whatever you choose to do, we will be with you. You're a part of our community and your success will be our joy.

Coming back as a Mentor
It's our hope that by appreciating the value of the mentors we will pair you with in your life, you will develop the desire, and passion to also contribute to someone's else life. In the subsequent annual bootcamp, we encourage you all to come back as mentors and contribute to the community you will be a part of.

Other Programs

High School Clubs

We support computer science clubs in high schools all over the country. We do so by providing them with resources to introduce them to computer programming and software management. Participating schools can compete in inter-schools min-hackathons where club members get together to build applications that address problems they identify in their communities. Best entries from individuals and schools are eligible to awards

Mentorship

Our community of mentors is there to support you as you begin to learn what it takes to pursue a career in tech. You will meet some of your mentors who will be in Kenya in June at the end of the bootcamp.
