About M-Soma

M-Soma is a Computer Science training program for Kenyan youth from under-resourced communities and schools. Founded in 2016, M-Soma aims to increase the number of skilled software engineers/developers and IT professionals in East Africa. 

Today in Kenya, very few young people get the opportunity to gain computer skills in high school due to a shortage of CS instructors and a lack of the necessary resources. There are many institutions offering these courses to high school graduates, but they are often either too expensive or don't offer courses that prepare scholars interested in careers in Computer Science.

M-Soma operates as a non-profit to ensure that even the most needy also get access to these opportunities to improve their lives and that of their communities.

Mission Statement

To provide young people from under-resourced communities and schools with the skills and community support they need to become professional software developers.

Our Programs

Bootcamp

We hold an annual 4-week bootcamp in June to introduce scholars to computer programming. Other courses taught include design thinking, entrepreneurship, among others. Scholars are matched with a mentor after the bootcamp.

Apply Now

High School Clubs

We support Computer Science clubs in high schools all over the country, by providing mentorship and learning resources. We organize a high school hackathon among participating schools during the second term of the year

Learn More

Mentorship

We connect our scholars to mentors who are scholars studying computer science, engineering, and related courses in universities in the US, Canada, South Africa, and Kenya. Mentors act as guides and career advisors
